Dhaka Snake Safety: Understanding Russell's Vipers

About Russell's Vipers

Russell's vipers (Daboia russelii) are venomous snakes found in many parts of Asia, including Dhaka, Bangladesh. They are typically not aggressive and play an important role in the ecosystem by controlling rodent populations.

Habitat

Russell's vipers in Dhaka are commonly found in areas with tall grass, bushes, and near water bodies like the Padma River and its tributaries. They may also be found in agricultural areas and around fallen trees or woodpiles.

Behavior

These vipers are nocturnal and try to avoid humans. They will only bite if they feel threatened or cornered. It's important to stay aware of your surroundings and avoid approaching them.

Venom

A Russell's viper bite can be serious and cause tissue damage, bleeding, and even kidney failure. However, there is effective antivenom available in hospitals throughout Bangladesh.

Safety Tips

  • Be Aware

    Stay alert, especially when walking in tall grass or overgrown areas at night.

  • Wear Protection

    Wear closed-toe shoes and long pants when outdoors.

  • Use Light at Night

    Carry a flashlight if you are going to be outside at night.

  • Leave Snakes Alone

    If you see a snake, do not try to approach or handle it. Leave the area calmly and call the National Helpline number 333 or inform the nearest forest department office.
